# Intro

![cover](/images/blog/missing_scientists.png)

Eleven people connected to America's most sensitive nuclear and aerospace programs. All either dead or vanished within three years. FBI is investigating, White House commented, Congress launched a formal probe.

Most coverage is either UFO bait or immediate dismissal. Both are lazy. What's actually here, when you look case by case, is eleven stories that are each independently and specifically weird before you even start connecting them.

Case by case breakdown of what happened, what the official record says and doesn't say, and two theories worth considering once you've seen the raw material.

# The Dismissal Argument

The mainstream pushback is essentially that America has thousands of scientists and people die all the time, so if you pick a category broadly enough, you'll always find deaths within it, and humans are generally wired to see patterns that aren't there.

This sounds reasonable and it generally works when variables are loose. Like "scientists dying" is extremely loose. That's hundreds of thousands of people. But that's not what we have here. Each additional filter applied makes the coincidence argument exponentially weaker.

**Filters stacking on this list are:**

- **Field** - not just scientists but nuclear, aerospace, plasma, asteroid defense, weapons systems.
- **Clearance level** - virtually everyone on this list had security clearance.
- **Geographic clustering** - four cases in Cali, four in New Mexico. These aren't random points on map, they cluster around specific facilities - JPL in Cali, Los Alamos and Kirtland in NM.
- **Institutional clustering** - Hicks, Maiwald, Reza, Grillmair are all from the JPL/Caltech institutional family. Four people from one institution.
- **Temporal clustering** - these aren't spread across a decade. The main cluster runs 2023-2026, roughly 36 months, with acceleration towards end.
- **Circumstantial clustering** - the most important factor. These deaths share characteristics: no disclosed cause of death, no autopsy, institutional silence, people leaving home with only a weapon and nothing else, etc.

The coincidence argument requires each variable to be independent. But when the same people, from the same institutions, in the same geo clusters, with the same clearance levels, die and vanish within the same window, with the same informational blackout applied uniformly, the variables aren't independent anymore.

So saying that it's pattern matching and pure statistical coincidence - is just straight bullshit and naive explanation of much deeper and correlated phenomenon.

# Why every single case is so fucking weird

## The Inverse Documentation Relationship

This is a specific kind of anomaly I noticed while going through the cases and it needs its own moment before the individual cases.

The cases with the most institutional presence — Loureiro, MIT director, two governments, hundreds of colleagues — have the most elaborate documented death. While the cases with the least institutional visibility (Maiwald, Hicks, both senior scientists at one of America's most important research facilities) have literally zero documentation.

In a normal world, that relationship is statistically reversed.

## The Supervision Paradox

Another anomaly worth talking about.

Loureiro is the only person on the entire list who died under institutional supervision. Hospital staff, ER, attending physicians, death certs, family present, two governments notified — the most witnessed death on the list by an enormous margin.

And he is also the only person on the list with a named killer.

And that named killer is also the only person connected to any of these cases who conveniently died alone, in a private location, in a different state, before anyone could speak to him.

This is extremely weird, because every other death on this list has no concrete explanation or suspects. The one death that required a narrative — because too many people witnessed it — got the most complete one available. Suspect named and all that.

The most witnessed death produced a suspect who died in the least witnessed way possible. Guy just offed himself in some bumfuck nowhere.

This story itself is a micro inversion sitting inside the macro inversion described above.

## The List Is Growing

Worth noting that the number isn't fixed. Congress is actively adding names. As of late April 2026 the official count had reached 11 with congressional letters going to the FBI, NASA, DOE, and DOD demanding briefings. The FBI confirmed it is "spearheading the effort to look for connections." Joshua LeBlanc, a 29-year-old NASA nuclear propulsion engineer, was added to the list after his case was flagged by Rep. Burlison and is detailed below.

## VANISHED — Monica Reza

![reza](/images/blog/reza.png)

**NASA JPL · Director of Materials Processing · Rocket & Missile Alloy Development · High Clearance**

This story is just so weird.

**SHE VANISHED 30 FEET FROM HER FRIEND.**

TL;DR she and her friend were hiking in Angeles National Forest. Her friend was around 30 feet ahead, looked back and saw Reza smiling, she even waved at her friend. Seconds later Reza literally ceased to exist. When her friend looked back there was nothing. No sound, no screaming, no footsteps. LITERALLY NOTHING. No body found.

He told me that he thought Monica was not on the mountain, that she had been "taken". He last saw the van at the 6000' Trailhead the day before Monica disappeared, and he hadn't seen it since. He thought maybe Monica was taken by this suspicious character driving the white van. If Monica had been jogging down the ridge and missed the turn, maybe she kept on jogging and ended up at Three Points. Maybe that van was there and the man offered her a ride. Maybe I watch too much true crime, but it's suspicious._
>
> _The man said he's worked at Waterman for 25 years. So if he thinks something looks suspicious, it's suspicious. I didn't catch his name. I asked him if he told the police. But they looked at me like I was wasting their time with second-hand info. I told them someone needs to drive up there and talk to this man because he had first-hand info. Obviously there is no way to confirm this, but I don't really see a reason why someone would lie about this either.

## DEAD — Nuno Loureiro

![loureiro](/images/blog/loureiro.png)

**MIT · Director Plasma Science & Fusion Center · Nuclear Fusion & Magnetic Reconnection · Government Funded Research**

The only supervised death on the entire list. Hospital, family, vigil, MIT president's statement, Portuguese foreign minister, Portuguese president — the most institutionally witnessed death by a massive margin.

Named suspect Claudio Valente — a Portuguese national with a decades-old grudge — conveniently died alone in a storage unit in New Hampshire before a single formal interrogation could take place, taking the entire narrative with him.

One detail worth adding that most coverage glosses over: the day before he shot Loureiro, Valente carried out a mass shooting on Brown University's campus that killed two students and wounded nine others. If the grudge narrative is true, Loureiro was always the primary target. So why the campus shooting first? Who drives to a university, opens fire on random students, then drives to another state to shoot a specific person the next day? The sequencing is strange and nobody has adequately explained it.

Most witnesses. Most documentation. Most elaborate cover story. And the one loose end that could unravel all of it, sealed in the quietest way possible.

## DEAD — Joshua LeBlanc

![leblanc](/images/blog/leblanc.png)

**NASA Marshall Space Flight Center · Aerospace Technologies Electrical Engineer · Nuclear Propulsion Projects · Mars Mission Programs**

29 years old. This case wasn't in the original list but was added to the congressional probe in late April 2026.

LeBlanc's body was found in the burned wreckage of his Tesla in rural Alabama in July 2025 after his vehicle struck a guardrail and several trees and caught fire. He had failed to show up for work that morning. His family reported him missing and told investigators he had left his phone, wallet, and dog at his apartment — which they described as completely out of character and the reason they initially suspected abduction.

Tesla Sentry Mode data showed his car had been stationary at Huntsville International Airport for approximately four hours before the crash, with no known travel plans.

No cause of death has been specified beyond the crash itself. The FBI is now examining the case as part of the broader pattern.

What distinguishes it from a straightforward accident: he was 29, working on nuclear propulsion for future Mars missions, left his phone and wallet behind before a fatal crash, and his car spent hours parked at an airport with no explanation. None of those details on their own are conclusive. Together they put it in the same bin as the others.

## DEAD — Amy Eskridge

![eskridge](/images/blog/eskridge.png)

**Institute for Exotic Science · Independent Researcher · Anti-Gravity Technology · UFO Disclosure Claims · No Institutional Clearance**

Ruled self-inflicted gunshot wound in 2022. Family confirmed chronic pain and declined to characterize her death as unusual.

What the family statement didn't address: Eskridge repeatedly and publicly stated she was being actively harassed and receiving threats specifically because of what she was working on and what she claimed to know about UFO disclosure. She said this on record, before she died. You can have chronic pain and also be genuinely threatened. The family explanation covers one and says nothing about the other.

Oldest case, most personal explanation, outside the institutional framework of the others. But the pre-death harassment claims on record are the one detail that doesn't get dismissed just because a medical explanation exists alongside it.
